How Many Children Are We Having?

A look at the world's Total Fertility Rate (TFR) compared to Life Expectancy

Families are having less children. Why? According to Our World in Data, several things happen as countries develop and become wealthier. Women have better access to education and are therefore prioritizing their careers over having children. In addition, the cost of raising a child increases which is a significant factor. In 2018, USA Today estimated that raising a child in the US was $233,610. That's a lot of money!

But the world's population is increasing? Although women seem to be having less children, in many countries, advancements in healthcare and better standards in living conditions have contributed to the increase in life expectancy. Generally speaking, from 1950 to 2015 the global trend has been that women are having fewer children, while the lifespan of the average person has increased. The World's Total Fertility Rate vs. Life Expectancy

About the Bubble Chart

The chart visualizes the total fertility rate (TFR), or the number of children that an average woman will have in her lifetime versus the average life expectancy per country. The data begins with the year 1950. Within 65 years, the averge life expectancy increases from 47.84 to 71.85 years, while the average TFR decreases by almost half from 5.48 to 2.76 children per woman.

Population and GDP

The population is depicted by the area of the circles. In other words, the larger the population, the bigger the circle. The GDP per capita is visualized in colors. The bluer in color a circle is, the higher the GDP per capita, and the lighter green in color a circle is, the lower the GDP per capita.

General Trend

Although this is a generalization, can you see that as a country's GDP increases, so does the life expectancy while the TFR decreases? Niger seems to be one of the exceptions. Its TFR in 1950 was 7.26, and in 2015 it was 7.29. Niger's GDP in 1950 was $1,148 and $895 in 2015. Though not by much, its GDP actually decreased, while its TFR increased.

About the Data

The total fertility rate, life expectancy, GDP per capita, and population data was taken from Our World in Data. This data was then slightly processed and formatted for the purposes of this project using MYSQL and Excel.
